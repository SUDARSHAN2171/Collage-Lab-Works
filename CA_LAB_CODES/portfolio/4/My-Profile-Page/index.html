    About Me

    In November, I was selected out of over 400 applicants to be part of a JavaScript class of 15 people at AW Academy. It was a 12-week, extremely intensive course with approximately 600 hours of coding. We received instruction in various subjects, including HTML, CSS, SASS, JavaScript, React, SCRUM, Git, Node.js, SQL, and Postgres.
    I changed careers because I wanted to experience self-development, challenges, problem-solving, and ultimately mastery. People have told me that I am a positive, proactive, creative and solution-oriented team player who has a lot to contribute. I believe the key to being a good developer is the desire and willingness to acquire new information. Additionally, communication is essential – understanding the client's desires and being able to communicate effectively.
    My CV shows experience in several industries due to my curiosity, desire for development, challenges, learning new things, and achieving the feeling of mastery. Therefore, I have decided that being a developer is the right job for me, and this is where I belong.
    Moving forward, I want to continue to develop my skills, learn new technologies and languages, and contribute to interesting projects. I started with JavaScript because I wanted to understand and contribute to both front- and backend development.
